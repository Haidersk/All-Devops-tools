## 🗣️ The Script

---

Good morning — thank you for having me.

My name is **Haider Shaikh**, and I'm a DevOps Engineer with 9 months of hands-on experience. I come from an Information Technology background, and what pulled me into DevOps was a simple curiosity — *I wanted to understand how great software gets delivered reliably, not just how it gets written.*

I built my foundation through focused training on Linux, AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, Jenkins, GitHub Actions, and monitoring with Prometheus and Grafana — and I backed every concept with real hands-on projects.

I then joined **Hisan Lab as a DevOps Intern**, where I worked for 9 months on actual production-style infrastructure. I built CI/CD pipelines using Jenkins and GitHub Actions that automated the entire delivery process from code push to deployment. I containerized applications with Docker and managed them on Kubernetes — including AWS EKS — handling deployments, autoscaling, and zero-downtime rolling updates. I also used Terraform to provision AWS infrastructure with modular, reusable code, and set up Prometheus and Grafana to give the team real visibility into system health.

One thing that sticks with me from that experience — I once traced a pod failure down to a single misconfigured environment variable using `kubectl logs`. Small fix, but it taught me that in DevOps, **debugging calmly and systematically is just as important as building things.**

I'm someone who thinks automation-first, stays composed under pressure, and communicates well across dev and ops teams.

I'm looking for a role where DevOps is taken seriously as a practice — and where I can keep growing while making a real contribution from day one.

I'd love to hear more about your team and what you're working on.

## ⚡ The Golden Rule

> **Specificity wins.**
> *"I fixed a CrashLoopBackOff by tracing a misconfigured env variable via kubectl logs"*
> is more impressive than listing 15 tools you've used.
> One real story beats a long resume read-out — every time.

---

## 🔁 How to Adapt This for Any Interview

You don't need a different script for every company.
Just swap **one sentence** based on the job description:

**For a company focused on AWS:**
> *"...and I'm especially excited about deepening my AWS infrastructure work in a production environment."*

**For a company using Azure or GCP:**
> *"...and I'm actively expanding into Azure, which aligns well with what your team is working on."*

**For a startup:**
> *"I thrive in environments where I can wear multiple hats and move fast — which is exactly why this role caught my attention."*

**For a large enterprise:**
> *"I'm drawn to environments with complex infrastructure where there's real depth to grow into."*

## 🏋️ Practice Checklist

Before your interview, make sure you can:

- [ ] Deliver the script in under 2 minutes without rushing
- [ ] Say the kubectl story naturally — not robotically
- [ ] Swap the last line based on the company you're interviewing at
- [ ] Make eye contact during the closing line
- [ ] End with a genuine smile and pause — let them respond

---

## 🚫 Things to Avoid

- ❌ Don't list every tool you know — pick the ones that matter
- ❌ Don't say *"I am a fresher"* — you have 9 months of real experience, own it
- ❌ Don't rush — silence and pauses sound like confidence
- ❌ Don't end flatly — the closing invite is what opens the real conversation
- ❌ Don't memorize word-for-word — know the story, not the lines
